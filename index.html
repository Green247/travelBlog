Destination 1 - Lagos

Lagos State is a vibrant and bustling coastal state located in the southwestern region of Nigeria. It is the most populous state in Nigeria, with a population of over 21 million people. Lagos is known for its rich culture, food, music, and nightlife, as well as its beautiful beaches and historical landmarks. The city of Lagos is home to many attractions, such as the National Museum, Lekki Conservation Centre, Freedom Park, and the famous Lagos Island Market. Lagos is also a hub for business and commerce in Africa, with a thriving economy and a cosmopolitan atmosphere.

The Top 6 must visit place in Lagos, Nigeria

Longest Canopy Walkway in Africa

Lekki Conservation Center is a lush natural reserve in Lagos, Nigeria, established in 1990 by the Nigerian Conservation Foundation (NCF). Spanning 78 hectares, it features a 401-meter long canopy walkway, nature trails, and picnic areas. Home to diverse wildlife and offering educational programs, it serves as a vital conservation and recreational destination for nature lovers and tourists alike.

National Museum Lagos

A cultural treasure trove showcasing Nigeria's rich history and heritage. It houses a vast collection of artifacts, including traditional crafts, artifacts, and ancient relics, providing insights into the country's diverse cultural heritage.

Nike Art Gallery

It is one of the largest art galleries in Nigeria, showcasing an extensive collection of traditional and contemporary African art. It features a diverse range of artworks, including paintings, sculptures, textiles, and beadwork, providing a vibrant display of African artistic expression.

Eko Atlantic City

A modern waterfront city built on reclaimed land, offering a futuristic glimpse of Lagos' urban development. It features skyscrapers, luxury hotels, pristine beaches, and state-of-the-art infrastructure, showcasing Nigeria's aspirations for a sustainable and modern metropolis.

National Theatre Lagos

A prominent cultural landmark and performing arts center that hosts various theatrical performances, music concerts, and cultural events. Its iconic design, featuring a distinctive white exterior and a dome-shaped structure, makes it a significant architectural masterpiece and a hub for Nigeria's vibrant arts and culture scene.

Lekki Beach

A popular beach destination for locals and tourists alike, offering pristine sandy shores and azure waters. It's a great spot for relaxation, water sports, and beachside activities, with numerous restaurants and bars providing a vibrant atmosphere for visitors to enjoy.

The Ultimate Lagos Street Food List: 6 Must Try Street Foods

Jollof Rice

A popular one-pot dish made with rice, tomato stew, and spices.

Moi Moi

Steamed bean pudding made with black-eyed beans, onions, and spices.

Akara

Deep-fried black-eyed bean fritters, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

Fried Rice

It is made from cooked rice that is stir-fried with vegetables, such as carrots, peas, and onions, and seasoned with soy sauce, oyster sauce, and other spices. Fried Rice is often served with grilled chicken or beef, and is a favorite among Lagosians.

Amala

It is a thick, brownish-black porridge made from yam flour and is often served with a spicy stew or soup, such as ewedu, gbegiri, or ogbono. Amala has a unique texture and is typically eaten with your hands by rolling small balls of the porridge and dipping them into the soup or stew.

Ofada Rice and Stew

Ofada Rice is a locally-grown rice variety that is unique to Nigeria. It is usually served with a stew made from palm oil, bell peppers, onions, and various spices. The stew is often made with different meats such as beef, goat, or chicken, and served with boiled eggs and fried plantains.

Destination 2 - Abuja

  • Home

    • Abuja is the modern and well-planned capital city of Nigeria, known for its impressive landmarks, diverse cultural attractions, scenic parks, and vibrant nightlife. The city offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for tourists exploring Nigeria.

    The Top 6 must visit place in Abuja, Nigeria

    Aso Rock

    This massive 400-meter rock is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Nigeria and is located in the heart of Abuja. It's a great spot for hiking, rock climbing, and enjoying the panoramic views of the city.

    Gurara Falls

    This beautiful waterfall is located just outside of Abuja and is a popular spot for swimming, picnicking, and hiking. Visitors can also learn about the local history and folklore surrounding the falls.

    Millennium Park

    This large park is a popular spot for jogging, picnics, and outdoor concerts. It's home to several gardens, fountains, and a large amphitheater.

    Zuma Rock

    This impressive rock formation is located just outside of Abuja and is said to have spiritual significance for the local people. Visitors can hike to the top for stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

    Abuja National Park

    This colorful market is a great place to shop for souvenirs and traditional Nigerian handicrafts. Visitors can watch local artisans at work and learn about the various cultural traditions.

    Arts and Crafts Village

    A popular beach destination for locals and tourists alike, offering pristine sandy shores and azure waters. It's a great spot for relaxation, water sports, and beachside activities, with numerous restaurants and bars providing a vibrant atmosphere for visitors to enjoy.

    The Ultimate Abuja Street Food List: 3 Must Try Street Foods

    Boli

    Grilled plantain is a popular street food in Nigeria, and is often served with spicy pepper sauce or peanut butter sauce. It can be eaten as a snack or as a side dish.

    Suya

    Spicy grilled meat served with a peanut-based sauce and garnished with onions and spices.

    Masa

    A traditional northern Nigerian snack made from ground rice that is mixed with yeast, sugar, and water to form a batter. The batter is then fried in small, pancake-like shapes and served with honey or a spicy sauce.

    Destination 3 - Port-Harcourt

  • Home

    • Port Harcourt is a vibrant city located in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria. With its rich cultural heritage, bustling markets, and lively nightlife, it's a must-visit destination for travelers who want to experience the authentic culture of Nigeria. The city is also surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, including rivers, beaches, and parks, making it a great place for outdoor activities and relaxation. Visitors can explore the city's museums, galleries, and cultural centers to learn about the history and traditions of the region. With its friendly locals, delicious food, and warm hospitality, Port Harcourt is sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler.

    The Top 6 must visit place in Port-Harcourt, Nigeria

    Port Harcourt Tourist Beach

    Located along the Atlantic Ocean, the Port Harcourt Tourist Beach is a popular spot for swimming, picnicking, and sunbathing. With stunning views of the ocean and a lively atmosphere, it's a great place to relax and enjoy the outdoors.

    Isaac Boro Park

    This urban park is a serene oasis in the heart of Port Harcourt. Named after the famous Nigerian activist, it features beautiful landscaping, walking trails, and a playground for children. Visitors can also enjoy a variety of recreational activities, such as basketball, tennis, and football.

    Rivers State Museum

    The Rivers State Museum is a must-visit for history buffs and culture enthusiasts. The museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history and culture of the region, including traditional clothing, crafts, and art.

    Port Harcourt Zoo

    The Port Harcourt Zoo is a popular attraction for families with children. Visitors can see a variety of animals, including lions, monkeys, and reptiles. The zoo also features a playground and picnic area.

    The Bonny Island

    Bonny Island is a beautiful island located off the coast of Port Harcourt. Visitors can take a ferry to the island and explore its beautiful beaches, fishing villages, and historic landmarks, including the King's Palace and the Bonny Museum.

    Cultural Center Complex

    The Cultural Center Complex is a vibrant cultural hub that showcases the art, music, and traditions of the region. Visitors can enjoy live performances of traditional music and dance, as well as browse local crafts and artwork. The complex also houses a library, theater, and museum.

    The Ultimate Port-Harcourt Street Food List: Three Must Try Street Foods

    Eba and Egusi

    Eba is made from cassava flour and is similar in texture to mashed potatoes. Egusi Soup is made from ground melon seeds and is often flavored with smoked fish or meat. It is a hearty and filling dish that is typically eaten with your hands.

    Fried Rice

    It is made from cooked rice that is stir-fried with vegetables, such as carrots, peas, and onions, and seasoned with soy sauce, oyster sauce, and other spices. Fried Rice is often served with grilled chicken or beef, and is a favorite among Lagosians.

    Efo Riro

    It is made from a variety of green vegetables, such as spinach, kale, or collard greens, that are cooked in a rich tomato-based sauce with onions, peppers, and other spices. Efo Riro is a nutritious and flavorful dish that is enjoyed by many in Lagos and across Nigeria.
